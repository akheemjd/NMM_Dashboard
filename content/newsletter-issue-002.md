**Subject:** USD/CAD at 1.3947. Cross-border math just changed.

---

## What moved this week

The Canadian dollar strengthened nearly 2 cents in a week. USD/CAD dropped from 1.4145 to 1.3947. That is a 1.4 percent move. For carriers running US lanes, that changes the math on every load.

A $5,000 US load converts to $6,973 CAD today. Last week, the same load was worth $7,072. That is $99 gone before you turn a wheel. If your last rate quote used 1.41, your margin has already shrunk.

The flip side: US fuel just got cheaper in Canadian dollars. Diesel at $4.25 USD a gallon cost $5.99 CAD last week. Today it costs $5.93. Small on one fill. Meaningful across a month of cross-border runs.

Fuel held steady. National diesel average is 171.9 cents a litre. NL at 182.5 cents, AB at 158.9. The 23.6 cent spread has not moved all month.

## 3 things to know

1. **A 2-cent loonie move changes border math.** Every cent the dollar gains costs about $10 CAD per $1,000 USD invoiced. Cross-border carriers billing in USD just took a hit.

2. **Fuel pressure is easing.** Diesel is trending down across most provinces. Add the stronger dollar and US fuel stops cost less this week than last. No province shows diesel trending up.

3. **The NL-AB gap is not closing.** A truck filling in St. John's pays $94 more than one filling in Calgary. If you run Atlantic lanes, that cost is structural. Your rates need to account for it year round.
