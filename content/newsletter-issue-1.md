**Subject:** NL diesel: 182.5¢. AB: 158.9¢. That 23.6¢ gap matters.

## What moved this week

Diesel prices held mostly steady across the country. The national average sits at 171.9 cents a litre. That number has not moved much. But the number that matters is the gap between provinces.

Newfoundland is paying 182.5 cents. Alberta is at 158.9. That is a 23.6 cent spread. On a 400 litre fill, a truck in St. John's pays about $94 more than one in Calgary.

If you run lanes into Atlantic Canada, you are paying the highest pump prices in the country. Your rates need to reflect that or you are losing money on every run.

The Canadian dollar is at 1.38 against the US dollar. Down slightly from last week. For carriers billing US loads in American dollars, the weaker loonie means more Canadian revenue on every cross-border run. If you are paying US fuel prices with a Canadian credit card, the weaker dollar works against you.

## 3 things to know

1. **The fuel spread is widening.** BC-AB was 19.6¢ last month. Now NL-AB is 23.6¢. Provincial tax differences are not changing. But when diesel is above $1.70, the same percentage gap hits harder.

2. **The loonie is helping some carriers and hurting others.** If you bill in USD, this is good. If you buy US fuel or equipment, this is not.

3. **Border crossings are normal for mid-July.** No major delays at the nine commercial crossings we track. CVSA inspection blitz is coming — dates on the dashboard.
