FAQ

Why is diesel so different across provinces?
Provincial fuel taxes are the main driver. BC adds carbon tax, transit levies, and a dedicated fuel tax. Alberta has no provincial sales tax and lower fuel levies. Transportation costs to remote areas (like Newfoundland) also push prices higher. The gap between BC and Alberta alone is 23.6¢/L right now — that's $94 on a 400-litre fill.
Where should I fuel up to save money?
Alberta consistently has the lowest diesel prices in Canada. Saskatchewan and Manitoba are the next cheapest. If you're running cross-country, fill up before leaving Alberta or Saskatchewan — prices jump significantly east of Manitoba and west of the Rockies.
How often do prices update?
Every 30 minutes from public fuel surveys across all ten provinces. Prices shown are the most recent available rack and pump prices.
Do these include carbon tax?
Yes. All federal and provincial taxes are included in the prices shown.
